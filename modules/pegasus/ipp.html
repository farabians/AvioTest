What is IPP?

The IPP (Information Processing and Perception) test evaluates your ability to handle multiple cognitive tasks simultaneously. You must watch dials and press the correct keys while performing mental math operations. The faster your reaction time, the higher your score.

1. Dial Tracking

  • There are three dials on the screen, each with a moving gauge.
  • Each dial is assigned a specific letter on your keyboard.
  • Dials have 3 zones: green, orange, and red.
  • When a gauge enters the red zone, press the assigned letter immediately.
  • Watch out for fakes: Dials may approach the red zone quickly then back off at the last second.
  • If you miss a red zone, the dial will flash red as a warning.
  • Note: Letter assignments may change during the test; the system will notify you when this happens.

2. Mathematics

  • You are given a starting number and a math rule (one of 4 possible rules).
  • Update the number step-by-step using the rule and the new numbers shown on screen.
  • The result of one operation becomes the base number for the next.
  • Enter your answer directly from the keyboard (no need to press ENTER).
  • If you make a mistake, the system will show the correct number and rule so you can continue from the right point.

How to Prepare

  • Easy: Start here to learn the rules and simultaneous tasks.
  • Medium: Similar to the hardest level in the real exam.
  • Hard: Meant to challenge you. Finishing it will give you strong confidence.
  • OFFICIAL Mode: Follows the exact rules of Phases 2 and 3 of the real exam.
